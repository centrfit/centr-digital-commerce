Where Things Go Wrong

Your biggest friction patterns revolve around Claude using wrong data sources or methodologies, requiring you to repeatedly correct its approach before getting usable results.

Wrong methodology requiring multiple corrections
Claude frequently starts with an incorrect analytical approach and you have to course-correct multiple times before it gets the methodology right. Providing explicit methodology requirements upfront (e.g., 'use cohorted LTV, not ARPU/churn estimates') could save significant back-and-forth.
  • You had to correct Claude twice on LTV methodology — first it missed months, then used ARPU/churn instead of cohorted LTV — burning multiple rounds before getting the right analysis
  • Claude repeatedly misframed the CAC/incrementality analysis for your executive deck, misquoting percentages and confusing organic cannibalization with paid lift, requiring several corrections before the framing was usable
Querying wrong data sources before checking context
Claude often searches the wrong database, file, or source before landing on the correct one — even when your own documentation already specifies where data lives. Strengthening your CLAUDE.md with explicit data source routing for common queries could reduce these misfires.
  • Claude searched SQLite instead of JSON files for forecast data, requiring multiple attempts to find the right source
  • Claude blamed the data source for a paid spend discrepancy instead of consulting your own context files and meeting notes that already documented the issue, prompting a sharp correction from you
Incomplete or incorrect initial output requiring rework
Claude's first pass frequently comes back with wrong data, missing date ranges, or phantom metrics that don't exist in your business, forcing you to QA and request fixes. Being more explicit about date ranges, metric names, and expected output in your initial prompts could reduce rework cycles.
  • Claude pulled only 2025 data when you also needed 2024, and a Google sign-out blocked sheet access — turning a simple data load into a multi-step recovery
  • Claude included engagement metrics that don't exist in your business (you track different engagement metrics), and on another occasion overwrote a strategy doc's guardrail questions with answers instead of preserving them
